About Me

Hello! My name is Christopher Willis. I'm a fellow at The Data Incubator and a recent graduate of Michigan State University, where I obtained my Ph.D. in Physics.

My research focused on performing searches for new elementary particles in the massive proton-proton collision datasets collected at the LHC at CERN in Geneva, Switzerland, where I lived for just under two years.

I'm fascinated by the process of deriving insights from data, and with more than six years of research experience, I'm excited to take what I've learned as a Physicist and apply it to solving impactful, real world problems in Data Science.

I was inspired to create this project because of my interest in Roman history. My dad introduced me to ancient coin collecting at a young age, and I wanted to create a tool that makes this activity more accessible.

Besides coin collecting, in my free time I like to travel, try new foods, and play racquetball.
